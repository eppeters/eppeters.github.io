Why Hand Tools Are Better Than Power Tools

This is just a cratchety old post I wanted to write because I couldn't stop thinking "People actually use these? That is so hard to believe" while actually being one of those people, guiding a board into my power thickness planer tonight. So much screaming and flying sawdust. I was way more attendant than participant.

Hand tools are better than power tools. This is due to the different relationship between the user and power between the two types of tool. Power tools require us to put our own mechanical force into preventing the machine from absolutely ravaging our work. We have to keep the router from going sideways, ensure we keep the board against the fence of the table saw, etc. In hand tool work, we certainly prevent our motions from carrying too far forward and marring the work, true. But at least we also apply something besides prevention force, or more aptly, "negative force." In hand work we provide both the push stroke and the pull stroke. We are the source of much of the energy, or at least we set it in motion with precision (indeed, when we are effficient, we don't cause the hammer to drop downward, but we do set it off to go downward just so). Using hand tools is much more fulfilling, at least to me.
