Broccoli, Cabbage, Onions, Basil, and Chamomile

Garden Plan

How much of each we want to plant in the garden

  • 8 Broccoli
  • 11 Chinese Cabbage
  • 40 Yellow Onions
  • 12 Red Onions
  • 96 Green Onions (table onions)
  • 24 chamomile
  • 28 Basil

Starting Counts

How many we're actually starting

  • 80 yellow onions and 80 white onions, for a total of 160 onions
  • About 40 yellow onions expected to bulb out
  • About 40 white onions expected to bulb out
  • About 80 onions expected as green onions
  • Purchased from Tractor Supply as bulbs, not seeds
  • 28 Chinese Cabbage
  • 21 Broccoli
  • 21 Chamomile
  • 35 Basil

Starting conditions

  • In front of a window
  • Big rack, not all gets light at once
  • Chamomile & Basil on a heat mat, but not cabbage and broccoli

Next Steps

  • Make a cold frame tunnel, probably using these instructions
  • Set Chinese Cabbage out after last frost
  • Fall broccoli and cabbage seedlings should be started about 2 months before first frost

Mistakes/Lessons Learned

  • I didn't know enough about onions. I bought both short and long day onions, but found that my zone requires something called intermediate onions, and if it's possible to plant the other types here, I don't know how. So I'd like to learn about that before next year.
  • Chinese Cabbage and broccoli say: "Start seeds for transplants 4-6 weeks prior to desired transplanting date... Set the plants in the garden about 4 weeks before last frost date in the spring..."
  • Next year, start seedlings around Feb 1-Feb 15
